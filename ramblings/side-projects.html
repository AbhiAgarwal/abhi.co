side-projects

A person who doesn't do a lot of projects usually lacks the first-hand understanding of what is hard to do and what is not. That is harmful because he or she will have a hard time understanding time frames and how long it takes to build things. Or how possible things are using technology. This is usually, but not always, the case. The core and simplicity of ideas aren't always important. Sometimes the important thing is to understand how long each part of the process or cycle takes, and how complex each part is.

Side projects don't always have to be technical side projects, but anything you work on the side besides your primary focus (usually school).
